Current Tutors

  • Michael Murphy

    Hello! I'm Michael Murphy, a grad student at USF, and I'm from Pasadena, California. I'm a pretty avid gamer and am a fan of a variety of books, movies, and television series. Before college I had never taken a Computer Science class in my life, but when I arrived Freshman year and had to pick classes I thought taking a programming class to fulfill my Math requirement would be more interesting than taking an actual Math class. I was actually pretty terrible at CS in the beginning, but I enjoyed the challenge and stuck with it. I learned a lot from each mistake I made along the way (and believe me, there were a lot of them) and I learned even more from the professors, TAs, and tutors who were always willing to take their time to help me out with whatever problem I was having, big or small. And now that I'm part of the CS Tutoring Center, I'm hoping to do the same. So if you are confused or stuck on anything (It happens to everyone, so don't be embarrassed. I can guarantee I've made much sillier ones) come see me and I'll help you out as much as I'm able to.

  • Diana Ly

    Hello everyone! My name is Diana and I'm from San Jose, California. I'm a senior, majoring in CS and minoring in neuroscience. The reason why I came to like computer science is that I enjoy the challenges it can present and how it can be applied to practically every field. My hobbies are playing video games, going on food adventures, and immersing myself in the world of anime and manga. I also enjoy helping others so feel free to ask me any questions.

Faculty Sponsors

  • Sophie Engle

    Professor Sophie Engle teaches CS 212 Software Development, CS 360 Data Visualization, MSAN 622 Information Visualization, and many other courses. Her research focuses on applied data visualization (especially to problems in computer security) and computer science education. Sophie is the current faculty sponsor.

  • Dave Wolber

    Professor David Wolber launched the CS Tutoring Center in Spring 2013. He is the author of App Inventor: Create Your Own Android Apps along with Hal Abelson, Ellen Spertus and Liz Looney from the App Inventor team, runs AppInventor.org which provides on-line, video-based teaching lessons, and teaches App Inventor in his CS 107 Computing, Mobile Apps, and the Web course. David began teaching App Inventor as part of Google's 2009 pilot program involving ten universities. In 2010, he received a grant from Google to work with the App Inventor team and authored the advanced tutorials that appear on the App Inventor site.
