  • Michael Murphy

    Hello! I'm Michael Murphy, a current Senior at USF, and I'm from Pasadena, California. I'm a pretty avid gamer and am a fan of a variety of books, movies, and television series. Before college I had never taken a Computer Science class in my life, but when I arrived Freshman year and had to pick classes I thought taking a programming class to fulfill my Math requirement would be more interesting than taking an actual Math class. I was actually pretty terrible at CS in the beginning, but I enjoyed the challenge and stuck with it. I learned a lot from each mistake I made along the way (and believe me, there were a lot of them) and I learned even more from the professors, TAs, and tutors who were always willing to take their time to help me out with whatever problem I was having, big or small. And now that I'm part of the CS Tutoring Center, I'm hoping to do the same. So if you are confused or stuck on anything (It happens to everyone, so don't be embarrassed. I can guarantee I've made much sillier ones) come see me and I'll help you out as much as I'm able to.

    Michael has been with the CS Tutoring Center since Fall 2013.

  • Alyssa Pohahau

    My name is Alyssa. I'm majoring in CS and minoring in Math. I currently help out with ACM and Women in CS. In the past, I interned at Rdio and Coderwall. I grew up in Santa Clara and have always been surrounded by computers and big tech companies. However, I was never seriously into computers as a kid. I wanted to declare a major when I was applying to colleges, so I spent a lot of time thinking about what I was good at and what kind of life/career I wanted after college. I played saxophone, but didn't love it enough to study music. I loved math, but I couldn't see myself having a career in it. After countless conversations with numerous people, I took advice from a close family friend and gave thought to CS. It seemed like a pretty ideal major: it's technical, I would be able to live in the Bay Area after college and it's an ever growing field. I declared it somewhat blindly, as I did not know exactly what it entailed, but I fell in love with it after my first semester. Besides computers, I'm into jazz, architecture, operas, going to museums and exploring the city. In addition to answering questions about assignments in my office hours, I'd be more than happy to chat about studying CS, resources to learn more about a specific field in CS (meetup groups/ online courses), or a common hobby we may have!

    Alyssa has been with the CS Tutoring Center since Spring 2013.

  • Kevin Porter

    My name is Kevin Porter. I grew up in San Diego playing soccer before coming to USF where I found a new passion in computer science. My passion for computer science stems from the fact that when you are done working on a computer science project you generally have something that you can use. This, to me, makes every computer science project interesting because you are working toward a finished product that does something rather than a paper or assignment that you will never look at again.

    Kevin has been with the CS Tutoring Center since Spring 2013.

Faculty Sponsors

  • Sophie Engle

    Professor Sophie Engle teaches CS 212 Software Development, CS 360 Data Visualization, MSAN 622 Information Visualization, and many other courses. Her research focuses on applied data visualization (especially to problems in computer security) and computer science education. Sophie is the current faculty sponsor.

  • Dave Wolber

    Professor David Wolber launched the CS Tutoring Center in Spring 2013. He is the author of App Inventor: Create Your Own Android Apps along with Hal Abelson, Ellen Spertus and Liz Looney from the App Inventor team, runs AppInventor.org which provides on-line, video-based teaching lessons, and teaches App Inventor in his CS 107 Computing, Mobile Apps, and the Web course. David began teaching App Inventor as part of Google's 2009 pilot program involving ten universities. In 2010, he received a grant from Google to work with the App Inventor team and authored the advanced tutorials that appear on the App Inventor site.
